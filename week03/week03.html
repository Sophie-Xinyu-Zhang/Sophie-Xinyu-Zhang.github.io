As We May Think

In the article As We May Think, Bushs stated how he imagined the in the future, soem kind of machine would be invented to "compress" the scientific knowledge into a more portable form, and thus can be spread in a faster and wider manner. It also predicted many technologies in the modern society, such as speech recognition and the World Wide Web.


Through reading As We May Think, I see how Bushs was ahead of his time by presenting such innovative ideas back in his time. As he said, in order for science to be useful, it is important to allow everyone have access, or be able to, use these science or the product of science in their daily lives.

Scientists have done tremendous work for this to become a reality. For example, looking around in our houses, we see there's washing machine, there's lamps, there's microwave, and many other electrical appliances. They are all real world applications of our knowledge in electricity.
